About Me
I am currently a Master's candidate in the School of Data Science at the University Virginia. While studying, I'm also working on research with Dr. Antonios Mamalakis. We are using deep neural networks to improve prediction of California’s winter hydroclimate.
Prior to returning to school, I worked at Guidehouse as an Operations Research Analyst, where I was part of a team supporting the Army Office of the Chief of Chaplains. I primarily worked on the effort to modernize their data storage and visualization capabilities, while also helping to design ad hoc research projects. Our longest running research project used a longitudinal study to assess the primary outcomes of the Chaplain Corps’ Spiritual Readiness Initiative.
Before that, I was a Research Fellow at Thriving Cities Group, a non-profit organization that helps community foundations across the country better utilize open data to address the needs of their community.
I strongly believe in a community informed approach to data practice that emerges out of collective action to address civic needs. Outside of the classroom and office I love books and movies that examine religious practice, cooking, photography, distance running, and watching football (both kinds).
