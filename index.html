About Me
I am currently a Masters candidate in the School of Data Science at the University Virginia. While studying, I am also working on a research with Dr. Antonios Mamalakis and three fellow students. We are using deep neural networks to enhance the ability to predict California’s winter hydroclimate.
Previously, I worked at Guidehouse as an Operations Research Analyst, where I was part of a team supporting the Army Office of the Chief of Chaplains. I primarily worked on the effort to modernize their data storage and visualization capabilities, while also helping to design ad hoc research projects. Our longest running research project designed a longitudinal study to assess the primary outcomes of the Chaplain Corps’ Spiritual Readiness Initiative.
Before that, I was a Research Fellow at Thriving Cities Group, a non-profit organization that helps community foundations across the country better utilize open data to address the needs of their community.
I strongly believe in a community informed approach to data practice that emerges out of collective action to address civic needs. Outside of the classroom and office I love books and movies that examine religious practice, cooking, photography, distance running, and watching football (both kinds).
